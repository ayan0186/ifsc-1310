Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is a web development strategy that starts with a basic, functional version of a website or web application, and then progressively enhances it with more advanced features and design elements. In other words, it ensures that all users, regardless of their device or browser capabilities, can access and use the core functionality of the site, while also providing a richer experience for those with more capable devices and browsers.

Progressive enhancement is important in modern web development because it promotes accessibility, resilience, and future-proofing. By building websites in layers, with the basic functionality available to all users, developers can ensure that the site remains usable even in less capable environments, such as older browsers or slow internet connections. Additionally, it allows for graceful degradation, meaning that if certain features are not supported, the site can still function without them.

Moreover, progressive enhancement fosters a mindset of inclusivity and accessibility, ensuring that all users, regardless of their device or abilities, can access and interact with the content. It also aligns with principles of web standards and best practices, promoting a more sustainable and scalable approach to web development.
